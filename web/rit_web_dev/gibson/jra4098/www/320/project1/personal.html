And all the rest...

To a biker, this is paradise.

I grew up in Vermont, the most beautiful state in the union. If you ever get a chance to visit Vermont, do so. Even in mud season, you'll find something to appreciate. Try some real maple syrup -- Aunt Jemima's can't compare.

When not working with computers, you're likely to find me reading. I adore good science fiction, such as Isaac Asimov's Foundation series, or Dune by Frank Herbert. Of course, my reading list is not restricted by that genre. Veracity by Mark Lavorato, Watchmen written by Alan Moore, and Rasselas by Samuel Johnson are worth your time. Indeed, they should demand it. I also have an enduring interest in cultural history, and especially how tech policy affects the shape of culture today. The smartphone industry provides a fascinating, modern-day case study in how policy can shape markets. If you're willing to look further back, Piracy by Adrian Johns and Where Good Ideas Come From by Steven Johnson are both excellent reads.

When I give myself the time, I'm also happy to play racquetball, go on a bike trip, or hike a mountain. Someday, I hope to bike across the country (or at least across Vermont). I know I can do it, too -- even with everything else to do, I've gone on at least one fifty-plus mile bike ride each year since 2010.

The one character trait I cannot stand in a person is boredom. Freedom from present pressures is something rare.
